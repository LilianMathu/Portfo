--Here's What I've Done so Far--

Mobile Mechanics

July 2020

The idea was inspired by the inconveniences I had with highway mechanics. I knew something had to be done to make the system more efficient. If your car has ever broken down in a remote area, you know how hard it is to get a good mechanic, who gets you back on the road in time and doesn't turn you into a gold mine. Well, Mobile Mechanics is a platform that connects stranded motorists to professional mechanics closest to them in a timely, convinient and affordable manner. The platform also includes an online auto spares shop that houses verified auto spares vendors. With professional mechanics and quality auto spares, we reduce the chances of your vehicle breaking down in the middle of nowhere. I developed the app using MERN stack and Africa's Talking SMS/USSD API for the offline platform.

Talk Box

June 2019

I was part of a team that created an assistive device (Talk Box) for children with communication problems. The talk box is used by children in Western parts of Kenya. We developed do-it-yourself (DIY) assistive technology prototypes that revolutionized how people with disabilities can access tools that will help them interact with the world. TalkBox allows users to communicate by touching images on an attached surface to play audio files stored within the system. The images and sounds can be customized during assembly, depending on an individual's unique needs. The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) has published the results in IEEE Pervasive Computing. The Pi is coded with Python.

My heart bleeds for the health sector. This led me to develop a web application that helps medical practitioners keep a detailed medical history for the patients.
